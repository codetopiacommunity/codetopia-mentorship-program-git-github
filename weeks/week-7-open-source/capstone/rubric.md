# Week 7 Capstone Rubric

Total: **100 points**. Pass = **70**.

Apply the same rubric to Path A (real PR) and Path B (simulated PR).

| Area | Points | What we look for |
|------|--------|------------------|
| **Target selection** | 10 | Clear justification, evidence of project-health triage, not the easy "tutorial repo". |
| **Issue fit** | 10 | The change is well-scoped — one logical thing, estimable in hours not days. |
| **Branch + commit hygiene** | 10 | Branch named per convention; commit(s) follow target's style; Conventional Commits (or DCO sign-off) if required. |
| **PR description — What** | 5 | Clear, one-to-three sentences. |
| **PR description — Why** | 10 | Explains the *motivation*, not just the symptom. Links to the issue if relevant. |
| **PR description — How** | 10 | Describes the approach at the right level of abstraction. |
| **PR description — Testing** | 10 | Concrete — commands run, results observed. "LGTM" is not testing. |
| **CONTRIBUTING.md adherence** | 10 | Followed the project's conventions (style, lint, tests, CLA/DCO). |
| **Writeup — process** | 10 | How you found the issue, triaged the project, decided on the change. |
| **Writeup — interactions** | 10 | Maintainer (or simulated) exchanges quoted with context and your response. |
| **Reflection** | 5 | One or two paragraphs that show growth, not just "I learned a lot." |

## Stretch (+50)

| Stretch | Points | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Real PR merged during week | 25 | Bonus for scope discipline. |
| Substantive review response + delivered change | 15 | Evidence in writeup: the comment, your reply, the follow-up commit. |
| Second PR opened (same or different project) | 10 | Must be a real PR, not a duplicate, not a churn PR. |

## Automatic fails

- Copying a PR description template without filling it in.
- Force-pushing over a reviewer's comments in a way that hides them.
- Being rude to a maintainer. We will see your public PR history; behave.
- Submitting someone else's PR as your own.
- Opening a real PR that wastes the maintainer's time (e.g., noise commits, untested changes, or a "hi just testing" change).

## Scoring guidance

- **90+**: Exceptional. Could serve as a model. The kind of PR maintainers remember you for.
- **80–89**: Strong. Minor issues — a rough edge in the writeup or the description.
- **70–79**: Passing. All the loop steps happened; polish is lacking.
- **Below 70**: Sent back for revision. Specific feedback required.

## Path A vs Path B scoring parity

Path B is **not** worth less than Path A if:

- The simulated PR is thorough.
- The writeup explains why the simulation was necessary.
- The `SIMULATED-REVIEW.md` imagines realistic maintainer comments (not softballs).

Mentees should not feel penalized for circumstances outside their control.
